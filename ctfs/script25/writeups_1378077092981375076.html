A detailed solution was provided directly:


Looking at the code, it first generates a random number, and prints the remainder when the first input is divided by the secret number.

To figure out exactly what the secret number is, we can first input -1. Then, we add 1 to the result to figure out the secret number.

This works because the closest multiple of the secret number, or s, that is less than or equal to -1 is -s. Therefore, the output will be the difference between those two numbers, which is s-1.

Now, just input the secret number, and you will get your flag.

Just Some Avocado (Misc/Forensics)

A detailed solution was provided directly:


1. The avocado is an encrypted zip file.
2. Use a zip password finder (e.g., https://github.com/agourlay/zip-password-finder) with rockyou.txt.
3. The password found is "impassive3428".
4. This extracts "staticnoise.wav" and "justsomezip.zip".
5. "justsomezip.zip" cannot be cracked with rockyou.txt.
6. Open "staticnoise.wav" in Audacity and switch to spectrogram view.
7. The spectrogram shows the text "d41v3ron".
8. Use "d41v3ron" as the password to unzip "justsomezip.zip".
9. Flag: scriptCTF{1_l0ve_d41_v3r0n}
